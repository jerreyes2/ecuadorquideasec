About us

Ecuadorquideas began in 1988 in the canton of Marcabeli in the province of El Oro, with the initiative of Mrs. Carmen Apolo Bustamante, which based on effort and sacrifice has managed to consolidate what is now known as Ecuadorquideas..

In its beginnings, Ecuadorquideas started as a refuge center for threatened orchids in danger of extinction, caused by indiscriminate logging and burning of forests turning them into simple pastures without any vegetation. For which a lease contract is made in the site of San Antonio of the Marcabeli canton, time later the construction of a greenhouse is made to later receive the endangered species.

Little by little, the idea of conserving the orchids was gaining strength, so in 1995, "Ecuadorquideas" bought a piece of land and built a greenhouse in the city of Machala, with the purpose of having the specimens in their most similar climate of origin and that is how they began to propagate the species and to obtain more of them, through the acquisition of the other companies of that time.


In 1997 the Ecuadorquideas management center had the need to expand its facilities, due to the capacity of management and reproduction by simple participation of the species and so it decided to look for a place with an ideal climate for orchids. The site chosen was "La Chilca" located in the canton of "Santa Rosa" in the province of "El Oro".


In 1998 Mrs. Carmen Apolo became a member of the ECUADORIAN ORGANIZATION OF ORCHIDOLOGY. With which the dream of continuing with the project was strengthened, since with it strong bonds were formed with the other partners.


In the year 2000 Ecuadorquideas was launched as a trade name and requested a patent permit from the FOREST DISTRICT OF THE PROVINCES OF EL ORO AND GUAYAS (Ministry of Environment). This was granted for a period of 1 year, thus Ecuadorquideas regulates its activities in front of public institutions, being able to make its first exports.


ECUADORQUIDEAS CHILCA has its facilities in the Province of "El Oro" in the Canton "Santa Rosa" Parish "Torata" Site La Chilca (Via A Piñas 200 meters from the central church of the site La Chilca).




The area is located within the so-called Tropical Rainforest in transition to Tropical Dry Forest, with rainfall ranging from 800 to 1200 mm per year, an average temperature of 27º C and drops to 12º C and a relative humidity between 70 to 90% depending on the season and the influence of sea winds due to the proximity to the sea, since the province has direct marine influence.

Plants

ECUADORQUIDEAS CHILCA, destined mainly to TROPICAL PLANTS mostly alocasias, anthuriums, colocasias, chlorospatha, elaphaglosum, geogenatus, marcgravia, monstera, philodendo, synogoniun, xanthosoma. It has a capacity of approximately 3000 plants, currently enabled for the species of the anthurium family that ECUADORQUIDEAS CHILCA owns.
